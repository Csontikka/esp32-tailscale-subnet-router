Device-wide network
Used as DHCP Option 12 on every uplink and as the default Tailscale machine name.
Rewrites the TTL hop-limit on every outgoing IPv4 frame on the STA side. Typical use: set to 64 to cloak a tethered downstream as the phone's own traffic when the upstream carrier throttles hotspot-detected packets. Applied live on save.

Tunnel MTU
—

AUTO picks 1420 when at least one peer is on a direct UDP path and 1280 when traffic is DERP-relayed (DERP adds ~60 B of HTTP framing on top of WireGuard). FIXED pins a user-supplied value in 576..1500. The effective MTU drives the WireGuard netif MTU, the AP-side TCP MSS clamp on every SYN, and the next-hop MTU reported in ICMP frag-needed replies.

About
MIT

esp32-tailscale-subnet-router — a single-board ESP32-S3 router that joins your IoT subnet directly to a Tailscale (or Headscale) tailnet, so remote peers can reach LAN devices behind a CGNAT or a phone hotspot without port forwarding.
